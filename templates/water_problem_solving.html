Water Problem Solving

When complications occur in a freshwater aquarium, it's important to troubleshoot correctly and quickly. Identify the symptoms your fish, plants, or aquarium are exhibiting, and match them with the most likely problem to pinpoint an effective solution.

RAISING AQUARIUM pH

In order for fish to thrive, the water needs to be kept at the same pH level that is best suited for their type and best mimics their natural habitat conditions. For instance, a pH of 6.8 – 7.2 is ideal for keeping an aquarium containing a variety of tropical fish, while a pH of 6.0 – 8.0 is best for goldfish.
