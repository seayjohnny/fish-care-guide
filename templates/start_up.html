STARTING THE AQUARIUM CYCLE

After starting a new freshwater aquarium or performing filter or tank maintenance on an established tank, the addition of beneficial bacteria are required to kick start the nitrogen cycle in your tank. This cycle, can take 2 to 6 weeks to fully establish in a new freshwater aquarium and will be necessary for the health of your fish. Without these beneficial nitrifying bacteria, toxic ammonia quickly builds up in the aquarium and will stress the fish. All-natural, API® QUICK START™ nitrifying bacteria formula immediately starts the nitrogen cycle, which converts toxic ammonia into nitrite, then into harmless nitrate to help prevent fish loss in your tank.

SETTING & HOLDING AQUARIUM pH

In order for freshwater fish to thrive, the pH in the aquarium must be maintained at a level similar to their natural habitat. pH can fluctuate with the addition of rocks, plants, and new fish to an aquarium, so it is important to test your levels weekly to ensure they remain in a healthy range for your fish.
